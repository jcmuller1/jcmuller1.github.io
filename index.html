About Me

My academic and professional careers thus far have been characterized by adaptation. After an unfulfilling venture into studying chemistry in college, I reconnected with my childhood infatuation with weather and transferred to the University of Oklahoma to study meteorology. Through my studies and research involvement, I developed a strong interest in long-term climate patterns and science communication. I accepted an offer to continue my studies at Texas A&M Univeristy, where I completed my master's degree and began working towards a Ph.D. Unfortunately, my fire had been dimmed by grinding away at a master's thesis during a time when leaving home was reserved for necessities only, and my office and bed being an arm's length apart in a small college apartment was no longer sustainable. I recalled what I enjoyed most about my master's program, working with and analyzing data, and decided to leave graduate school to enter the analytics workforce at a hospitality management company. After a few months of getting back on my feet, I enrolled in a Data Analytics and Visualization bootcamp through the University of Texas at Austin, where my passion was reignited. My current career goal is to work with data in engaging industries that provide opportunities for further training, education, and growth. Bonus points if I can combine my love for playing and watching my favorite sports and esports with the skills I have developed along the winding path that has been my professional life.

When I'm not working, you can find me running the table at bar trivia, playing video games, on the tennis or volleyball courts, or in the pool. Revenue Strategy Tools

I am solely responsible for the development and upkeep of multiple tools used by the Aimbridge Hospitality Revenue Strategy team to collect and visualize data from numerous platforms. These tools are instrumental in decision-making within the department, as they save revenue managers and directors valuable time that can instead be used to formulate and implement strategy. I take on new tool, tool enhancement, and tool repair requests, determine the priority, and institute a plan of action accordingly. We are in the process of transitioning to Power BI, including an automated extract/transform/load process for reports received on a regular basis via Power Automate. Due to the proprietary nature of these tools, an example cannot be provided.

Technologies used: Power BI, Power Automate, ETL, Excel, VBA
