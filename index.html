About Me

My academic and professional careers thus far have been characterized by adaptation. After an unfulfilling venture into studying chemistry in college, I reconnected with my childhood love of weather and transferred to the University of Oklahoma to study meteorology. Through my studies and research involvement, I developed a strong interest in long-term climate patterns and science communication. I accepted an offer to continue my studies at Texas A&M Univeristy, where I completed my master's degree and began working towards a Ph.D. Unfortunately, my fire had been extinguished by grinding away at a master's thesis during a time when leaving home was reserved for necessities only, and my office and bed being an arm's length apart was no longer sustainable. I recalled what I enjoyed most about my master's program, working with and analyzing data, and decided to leave graduate school to enter the analytics workforce at a hospitality management company. After a few months of getting back on my feet, I enrolled in a Data Analytics and Visualization bootcamp through the University of Texas at Austin, where my passion was reignited. My current career goal is to work with data in either the sports or gaming realm, combining my love for playing and watching my favorite sports and esports with the skills I have developed along the winding path that has been my professional life.

When I'm not working, you can find me running the table at bar trivia, playing video games, on the tennis court, or in the pool. I am heavily involed in the competitive Pokemon scene as a player and content creator; I qualified for the World Championships thrice, provide color commentary on tournament streams, and co-host a podcast and talk show. I am a big Atlanta Braves fan and enjoy attending concerts and making music whenever possible. I am currently located in the unpleasantly hot Dallas, Texas.
